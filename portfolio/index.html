Research Work

Publication in Process
🎓 ICAISS - 2026

Evaluating Air, Land and Sea based Nature Inspired Algorithm for Travelling Salesman Problem

Conference

Fourth International Conference on Augmented Intelligence and Sustainable Systems (ICAISS-2026)

My Role

Research, Analysis, Documentation, and Presentation

Research Focus

This study evaluates the performance, speed, and accuracy of Nature-Inspired metaheuristic optimization algorithms categorized by environmental domains (Air, Land, and Sea) in solving the NP-hard Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP).

Utilizing execution time and execution cost metrics, this study benchmarks their limits under diverse structural scales.

Key Findings & Results

Delivered comprehensive statistical performance comparative data showing that ocean-based heuristics (Whale Optimization) and land-based (Grey Wolf Optimizer) exhibit superior convergence speeds and lower search costs.

These approaches outperformed air-based models such as Dragonfly and Firefly algorithms on complex node sets.

Algorithms Used for Study

Firefly Algorithm Dragonfly Algorithm Grey Wolf Optimizer Whale Optimization Algorithm
